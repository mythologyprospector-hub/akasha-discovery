# Discovery Process

Akasha discovery follows a structured pipeline.

## 1 — Observation

Read canonical knowledge from **akasha-world**.

Inputs may include:

- entities
- domains
- relationships
- constraints

---

## 2 — Pattern Analysis

Search for:

- incomplete classifications
- missing symmetry
- broken analogies
- unbalanced structures

---

## 3 — Gap Detection

Possible signals of a discovery gap:

- pattern discontinuity
- structural imbalance
- incomplete taxonomy
- missing transitional states

---

## 4 — Candidate Generation

Create one or more candidate hypotheses.

Examples:

- missing entity
- missing relationship
- missing phase/state
- missing constraint

---

## 5 — Candidate Recording

All candidates are recorded in `/candidates`.

Each candidate must follow the **candidate format specification**.

---

## 6 — Human Review

Human stewards evaluate the candidate.

Possible outcomes:

- accepted
- rejected
- archived
- requires further experimentation

---

## 7 — Integration

Accepted discoveries are integrated into **akasha-world**.
